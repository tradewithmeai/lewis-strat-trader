## Trump tweets and equity/bond/crypto market volatility: the Volfefe Index (JPMorgan 2019) and its academic anchor (Klaus & Koser 2021), Nishimura/Dong/Sun (2021) on tweet sentiment, RV and jumps, Born/Myers/Clark (2017) event study, US-China trade-war tweet studies (Burggraf/Fendel/Huynh 2020; Gjerstad et al. 2021), the intraday VIX/policy-clustering study (Perico Ortiz 2023), the Bitcoin sentiment study (Huynh 2021), and the directly-named prior approach (Krause SSRN, hand-curated daily event studies).

- **JPMorgan Chase (Munoz, J. et al.), 'Volfefe Index', launched 9 Sep 2019. Practitioner/sell-side product, not peer-reviewed. Reported in Bloomberg (2019-09-09) and CNBC (2019-09-08). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volfefe_index**
  - Finding: JPMorgan built an NLP index on ~14,000 Trump tweets and reported that Trump tweets explain a measurable fraction of intraday implied-rate volatility for 2y and 5y US Treasuries; of ~4,000 non-retweet tweets during market hours (2018-on), only ~146 moved rates. Market-moving keywords: China, billion, products, Democrats, great, dollars, tariffs, trade.
  - Relevance: This is the origin of the whole 'tweets -> second-moment' literature and the namesake our slice is built around, but it is a sell-side product with no public methodology, daily/implied-vol focus on Treasuries, first-term Twitter era. We instead test realized vol on crypto + a macro panel with a pre-registered, fully documented method. The specific '2y/5y implied vol' quant claim is from news coverage, not a verifiable paper — cite cautiously and anchor the concept to Klaus & Koser below.
- **Klaus, J. & Koser, C. (2021). 'Measuring Trump: The Volfefe Index and its impact on European financial markets.' Finance Research Letters, 38(C), 101447. DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2020.101447**
  - Finding: Using the Volfefe Index as a 'Trump tweet factor' and 20-day rolling-window regressions over 4 Apr 2018 - 2 Oct 2019, the factor adds predictive power for returns on major European indices (DAX, CAC, IBEX, FTSE, ATX, SMI, PSI, ISEQ); the effect is non-linear, heterogeneous across countries, and time-varying.
  - Relevance: This is the citable academic anchor for the Volfefe concept (use this rather than the news snippet). It is DAILY, first-moment (returns) on European equity indices, first-term era. We differ on every axis: intraday/hourly crypto + daily US macro, second-moment primary (RQ1), text-only signal we build ourselves rather than ingesting JPMorgan's black-box index, and a tradeable-edge test.
- **Nishimura, Y., Dong, X. & Sun, B. (2021). 'Trump's tweets: Sentiment, stock market volatility, and jumps.' Journal of Financial Research, 44(3), 497-512. DOI: 10.1111/jfir.12248**
  - Finding: Studies the SECOND-MOMENT response of the US stock market to Trump tweets using daily realized volatility decomposed into continuous and jump components. Finds tweeting positively and significantly raises volatility and jumps in the later sample period; the RV response exceeds the continuous-vol response because of the jump component, implying tweets raise jump tail risk that cannot be hedged without paying extra premium.
  - Relevance: The closest conceptual cousin to our RQ1 (vol) and the canonical 'tweets raise the second moment / jumps' result. Crucially, it does NOT separate the tweet effect from pre-existing volatility clustering — it regresses vol/jumps on tweeting without a trailing-vol control. Our RQ1 directly addresses that conflation: we test elevated 1-4h forward RV BEYOND trailing-24h vol via an era + trailing-vol-quintile-matched bootstrap null (the 24h effect was itself just clustering). Also: daily vs our hourly; US equities vs crypto; first-term Twitter vs 2022-26 Truth Social.
- **Born, J. A., Myers, D. H. & Clark, W. J. (2017). 'Trump tweets and the efficient Market Hypothesis.' Algorithmic Finance, 6(3-4), 103-109. DOI: 10.3233/AF-170211**
  - Finding: Daily event study (single-index market model, abnormal returns vs S&P 500) on 15 tweets targeting 10 named firms over the president-elect window (9 Nov 2016 - 20 Jan 2017). Positive/negative-content tweets elicit positive/negative abnormal returns concentrated open-to-close on day 0, becoming insignificant within ~5 trading days; volume spikes (~+87%) and Google-search patterns suggest noise-trader-driven, transitory reactions.
  - Relevance: NOTE: the study under review cites this as 'Born/Myers/Clary' — the correct third author is Clark. This is FIRST-moment (directional CARs), daily, firm-level, tiny n (15 tweets), first-term. It is the methodological template (event study + abnormal returns) our RQ2 generalizes, but we use the full ~33k-post corpus, intraday horizons, index/crypto-level (not single-name) targets, and multiple-testing correction. Their 'transitory, noise-driven' framing also motivates our RQ3 net-of-cost test.
- **Gjerstad, P., Meyn, P. F., Molnar, P. & Naess, T. D. (2021). 'Do President Trump's tweets affect financial markets?' Decision Support Systems, 147, 113577. DOI: 10.1016/j.dss.2021.113577**
  - Finding: Uses precise tweet timestamps with HIGH-FREQUENCY (intraday) financial data and LDA topic modeling. After Trump tweets, the US stock market declines and both uncertainty and trading volume increase; effects are strongest for the 'trade war' topic and for tweets containing 'products' and 'tariff'. The Chinese market also falls on trade-war tweets while gold rises.
  - Relevance: One of our two closest INTRADAY comparators and the strongest precedent for timestamp-anchored, topic-classified tweet analysis (their LDA topics parallel our frozen keyword taxonomy; their 'trade war' dominance parallels our 2025-26 tariff-regime caveat). But it is descriptive/associational (both moments, no OOS, no cost) on equities/gold, first-term era. We add a text-only causal-novelty signal, purged/embargoed walk-forward ML, FDR + Romano-Wolf correction, and a net-of-cost tradeable test on crypto — none present here.
- **Burggraf, T., Fendel, R. & Huynh, T. L. D. (2020). 'Political news and stock prices: evidence from Trump's trade war.' Applied Economics Letters, 27(18), 1485-1488. DOI: 10.1080/13504851.2019.1690626**
  - Finding: Classifies 3,200+ Trump tweets and finds US-China trade-war tweets NEGATIVELY predict S&P 500 returns and POSITIVELY predict the VIX; Granger causality is one-directional (tweets -> returns/VIX), with sectoral heterogeneity by China trade-intensity.
  - Relevance: Directly on the US-China trade-war-tweet slice and spans BOTH moments (returns + VIX), making it a precedent for our RQ2 (direction) and RQ1 (vol). It is daily, equity/VIX, first-term, with no trailing-vol control, no OOS holdout, and no transaction-cost test. We extend to intraday crypto, add the trailing-vol-matched null, and the cost-net Sharpe-CI edge test. (Author order differs between the SSRN preprint listing and the published article — defaulting to the published Applied Economics Letters order.)
- **Perico Ortiz, D. (2023). 'Economic policy statements, social media, and stock market uncertainty: An analysis of Donald Trump's tweets.' Journal of Economics and Finance, 47, 333-367. DOI: 10.1007/s12197-022-09608-5**
  - Finding: Double unsupervised ML (LDA + hierarchical semantic clustering) on ~7,376 tweet/retweet observations (31 Dec 2015 - 21 Oct 2019), with an INTRADAY event study on the VIX measured in 5-minute bars from 15 min pre- to 5 h post-tweet. Foreign-policy/trade tweets raise uncertainty most (~+0.06pp peak); monetary-policy (high-retweet) and immigration tweets also significant; fiscal/healthcare negligible.
  - Relevance: Our single closest methodological analogue: intraday, second-moment, topic-clustered, multi-hour post-event window (their 5h ~ our 1-4h). Differences are still sharp: it measures the VIX (an equity-implied-vol gauge) not realized crypto vol; it has no trailing-vol-matched null, no purged/embargoed walk-forward, no OOS net-of-cost trade; and uses engagement (retweets) as a moderator, which we deliberately exclude because counts are not known at post time. First-term Twitter vs our 2022-26 Truth Social.
- **Huynh, T. L. D. (2021). 'Does Bitcoin React to Trump's Tweets?' Journal of Behavioral and Experimental Finance, 31(C), 100546. DOI: 10.1016/j.jbef.2021.100546**
  - Finding: On 13,918 tweets (Jan 2017 - Jan 2020, extended through COVID), DAILY analysis with Granger causality finds NEGATIVE tweet sentiment predicts Bitcoin returns, trading volume, realized volatility, and jumps; only negative sentiment Granger-causes volatility, with time-varying dependence.
  - Relevance: The key crypto precedent and direct support for our crypto focus and second-moment (RQ1). It is daily, single-asset (BTC), sentiment-only, first-term Twitter, and associational. We extend to hourly BTC/ETH/SOL (noting their ~0.92 co-movement = ~one factor), a richer text-only signal (FinBERT + LLM stance/conviction + taxonomy + causal novelty), a trailing-vol-matched null, and a tradeable edge test.
- **Krause, D. (2025). 'Tariffs, Tokens, and Turmoil: The Market Fallout from Trump's Policy Uncertainty' (SSRN 5210267); and 'The Trump Effect: An Event Study of Asymmetric Bitcoin Market Responses to Political News' (Nov 2025, SSRN 5798822); and 'Trump-Era Cryptocurrency Policy Events and Bitcoin Returns' (Dec 2025, SSRN 5863983). Marquette University.**
  - Finding: Daily event studies on HAND-CURATED sets of ~30 political/macro/crypto-policy events (Jan 2024-Nov 2025). Average abnormal return significant only at ~10%; a few uncertainty-resolving events drive most of the effect (e.g. Election Day 2024, Nov-2024 Trump-industry meeting +5.63%); event categories ranked Political-Symbolic 4.49% > Market-Access 2.51% > Regulatory-Relief 2.21% > Executive-Action 0.81%.
  - Relevance: The prior approach the study explicitly names and our tightest head-to-head. Krause hand-picks events (selection + look-ahead exposure), works daily, and reports CARs. We instead classify the FULL ~33k-post corpus with TEXT-ONLY labels (no forward returns enter any label, by construction), at intraday/hourly resolution, with purged/embargoed CV and multiple-testing correction — directly addressing the curation/circularity weakness while overlapping his exact second-term tariff regime.

**Gap we fill:** Relative to this slice, the gap is threefold and concrete. (1) TRADEABLE EDGE NET OF COST: every prior paper here is descriptive/predictive — event-study CARs (Born/Myers/Clark, Krause), Granger causality on returns/VIX (Burggraf/Fendel/Huynh), realized-vol/jump regressions (Nishimura/Dong/Sun, Huynh), or VIX-response event studies (Perico Ortiz, Gjerstad et al.). None tests whether a mechanical post-event rule beats zero NET of round-trip cost on a time-ordered OOS holdout with a block-bootstrap Sharpe-CI lower bound > 0. RQ3 is the clean, unfilled gap. (2) TWEET EFFECT SEPARATED FROM VOL CLUSTERING: the canonical second-moment results (Nishimura/Dong/Sun; Volfefe; Huynh) show tweets co-occur with elevated volatility but never net out pre-existing volatility clustering; our RQ1 tests forward RV BEYOND trailing-24h vol using an era + trailing-vol-quintile-matched bootstrap null, and we explicitly find the 24h effect was clustering while a smaller 1-4h effect survives. (3) PLATFORM/ERA/ASSET/CORPUS/RIGOR: prior work is overwhelmingly daily, first-term TWITTER, 2016-2020, on US equities/VIX/Treasuries (only Perico Ortiz and Gjerstad et al. are intraday, and both on equity/VIX, associational, no OOS); we use intraday/hourly crypto (BTC/ETH/SOL) plus a daily macro panel, on the FULL ~33k-post Truth Social corpus (2022-2026, second-term tariff regime), with text-only non-circular labels, purged+embargoed walk-forward CV, and Benjamini-Hochberg FDR + Romano-Wolf correction across the test grid. Versus the directly-named prior approach (Krause), the head-to-head gap is: full-corpus text-only classification + intraday resolution + multiple-testing correction, instead of hand-curated daily event lists exposed to selection/look-ahead.

## The David Krause (Marquette University) SSRN cluster on Trump and crypto — daily, hand-curated-event studies on Bitcoin. Five papers verified against primary SSRN abstract pages via headless browser (Cloudflare-gated; WebFetch 403'd). The three slice-named papers (Trump-Era Policy Events; Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds; Tariffs, Tokens, and Turmoil) plus two companions (The Trump Effect; Meme Coins and the Trump Effect). All are short single-author working papers (10-19 pp), in-sample, no out-of-sample/cost/multiple-testing controls.

- **Krause, David (2025), 'Trump-Era Cryptocurrency Policy Events and Bitcoin Returns: An Event Study Analysis.' SSRN Working Paper, dated 4 Dec 2025, posted 12 Dec 2025, 17 pp. https://ssrn.com/abstract=5863983 (doi:10.2139/ssrn.5863983). Marquette University. PRIMARY-SOURCE VERIFIED via SSRN abstract page.**
  - Finding: DAILY market-adjusted event study on 14 HAND-CURATED Trump-era crypto policy events (Jul 2024-Dec 2025), classified into 4 types (Regulatory Relief, Executive Actions, Market Access, Political Symbolic). Mean 3-day cumulative abnormal return (CAR) = 2.22% (p=0.038); 78.6% of events positive (=11/14). CARs differ by category (ANOVA F=4.44, p=0.032): Political Symbolic 4.49% (p=0.059), Regulatory Relief 2.21% (p=0.020, 100% positive), Market Access 2.51%, Executive Actions 0.81%. Concludes markets discriminate among event types; supports an aggregate 'Trump trade'.
  - Relevance: Closest analog and cleanest contrast. SAME asset (BTC) and phenomenon (Trump policy -> crypto). DIFFERS on every axis our study fixes: (1) event selection - 14 hand-curated, ex-post analyst-chosen events vs our text-only classification of the FULL ~33k Truth Social corpus, removing event-selection look-ahead; (2) frequency - DAILY 3-day CAR vs our hourly 1/4/24h bars; (3) NO out-of-sample holdout, NO transaction-cost net test, NO multiple-testing correction (many raw-p category t-tests, several marginal), NO purged/embargoed CV. Reports in-sample CARs, not a tradeable edge. Does NOT test forward vol (our RQ1). Its categories resemble our frozen taxonomy, but ours is pre-committed and return-blind.
- **Krause, David (2025), 'The Trump Effect: An Event Study of Asymmetric Bitcoin Market Responses to Political News.' SSRN Working Paper, dated 24 Nov 2025, posted 27 Nov 2025, 10 pp. https://ssrn.com/abstract=5798822 (doi:10.2139/ssrn.5798822). PRIMARY-SOURCE VERIFIED via SSRN abstract page.**
  - Finding: Tests Krugman's 'Bitcoin = Trump trade' thesis with DAILY returns (Jan 2024-Nov 2025) in three stages: (1) correlation of BTC returns with daily Trump approval-rating changes - near-zero (r=0.04), NO continuous predictive power; (2) factor regression - BTC is a high-beta risk asset (beta=0.97 vs S&P 500, p<.001); (3) market-adjusted event study on 30 political/macro events - average abnormal return significant only at 10% (p=0.079). ASYMMETRY: that average masks heterogeneity; a small subset of 'uncertainty-resolving' events drives disproportionate returns (15 Nov 2024 Trump-industry meeting +5.63%; Election Day 2024 +1.07%). Concludes BTC reacts episodically at rare regulatory-expectation junctures, not continuously; argues this challenges semi-strong efficiency.
  - Relevance: Most relevant to our RQ2 (sign) and RQ3 (edge). Its core message - a CONTINUOUS political signal has near-zero predictive power (r=0.04) while effects concentrate in a few episodic events - is exactly the prior we must beat or qualify: we ask whether a continuous automated text signal over ALL bursts predicts sign/vol beyond those rare hand-picked junctures. DIFFERS: its 'signal' is daily approval-rating change (external poll), not Trump's own posts; daily not hourly; in-sample, no OOS/cost/FDR. Its episodic finding motivates our caveat that the 9 Apr 2025 'GREAT TIME TO BUY' post is an n=2 case study, not a class. NOTE: this and 5863983 are near-duplicate same-author event studies; cite distinctly - 2.22%/p=0.038/78.6% belong ONLY to 5863983; r=0.04/beta=0.97/p=0.079 ONLY to 5798822.
- **Krause, David (2025), 'Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds: An Empirical Study of Digital Asset Reactions to Presidential Crypto Advocacy.' SSRN Working Paper, dated 4 May 2025, posted 5 May 2025, 19 pp. https://ssrn.com/abstract=5241126 (doi:10.2139/ssrn.5241126). JEL G10/G14/G15/E44/C10/D53/P20. PRIMARY-SOURCE VERIFIED via SSRN abstract page.**
  - Finding: Dual methodology on the impact of crypto advocacy by Trump's family (World Liberty Financial, $TRUMP meme coin): (1) one-year return-and-risk evaluation of BTC vs S&P 500, with Ethereum and gold comparators; (2) a self-described 'high-frequency event study' splitting excess returns into event vs non-event periods and t-testing whether average excess returns are higher in event windows. Finds BTC notably susceptible to political signaling from figures with regulatory sway and financial interest.
  - Relevance: The one Krause paper NOT purely daily - billed 'high-frequency,' so do NOT call the whole cluster daily-only (a reviewer would puncture that). CAVEAT: the SSRN abstract does NOT state the bar granularity (minute/hourly/intraday); 'high-frequency' is unconfirmed below daily from public metadata. Still DIFFERS fundamentally: studies family/advocacy events (hand-identified) via a simple event-vs-non-event excess-return t-test, not a continuous classified-text signal; no purged CV, no cost-net OOS Sharpe-CI, no FDR/Romano-Wolf, no forward-vol test. Differentiate on selection + look-ahead + OOS rigor rather than frequency alone.
- **Krause, David (2025), 'Tariffs, Tokens, and Turmoil: The Market Fallout from Trump's Policy Uncertainty.' SSRN Working Paper, dated ~8-11 Apr 2025. https://ssrn.com/abstract=5210267. (Via SSRN search + Pension Policy International summary; NOT opened in browser this session.)**
  - Finding: Indexed/descriptive return analysis (NOT a formal event study) over Apr 2024-7 Apr 2025 across US equities, the 'Magnificent Seven', cryptocurrencies, gold, and bonds. Gold/defensive assets outperformed as safe havens; Mag-7 equities and crypto saw sharp post-inauguration (Jan 2025) drawdowns. Attributes elevated volatility and lost investor confidence to 'inconsistent economic governance' / policy uncertainty.
  - Relevance: Most relevant to our RQ1 (vol) but only QUALITATIVELY - observes elevated vol/turmoil under policy uncertainty descriptively; does NOT predict forward realized vol, does NOT control for trailing vol, is not even an event study. So our RQ1 (does a market-relevant burst predict 1-4h forward realized vol BEYOND trailing-24h vol, vs an era + vol-quintile matched bootstrap null) has NO analog in this paper or the cluster. Also documents the single-regime problem (2025 tariff war dominates), mirroring our caveat that our sample is dominated by the 2025-26 tariff war.
- **Krause, David (2025), 'Meme Coins and the Trump Effect: Deregulation, Speculation, and the Future of Political Cryptocurrencies.' SSRN Working Paper. https://ssrn.com/abstract=5162499. (Metadata from SSRN search; not opened in browser this session.) Part of a broader Krause descriptive series incl. 'Deregulation, Bitcoin, and the Trump Era' (5023943), 'Rewriting Crypto Regulation' (5119438), 'Trump's Crypto Playbook' (5158702), 'Bitcoin Valuation: Sentiment, Trump-Era Politics' (5227526), 'Strategic Bitcoin Reserve' (5177299).**
  - Finding: Qualitative/policy analysis of $TRUMP-style meme coins, deregulation, and speculation around political cryptocurrencies under the Trump administration; part of Krause's prolific descriptive series rather than an econometric event study.
  - Relevance: Establishes that the Krause cluster is overwhelmingly DESCRIPTIVE/POLICY commentary plus a handful of small in-sample daily event studies - none uses NLP/text classification of primary social-media posts, none uses machine learning, none does purged walk-forward OOS testing with transaction costs, and none derives a directional signal from the text itself. This is the cluster-level gap our study fills. Cite collectively to show breadth without padding individual claims.

**Gap we fill:** Relative to the entire Krause slice, our study fills four gaps none of these papers touch, ordered by strength: (1) EVENT-SELECTION DISCRETION REMOVED - Krause uses 14-30 hand-curated, analyst-chosen events; we classify the FULL ~33k-post Truth Social corpus with a frozen, pre-registered text pipeline (FinBERT + gpt-4.1-mini stance/conviction + keyword taxonomy + causal novelty), so 'events' are not chosen ex-post by an analyst who already knows which dates moved markets. (2) NO LOOK-AHEAD / CIRCULARITY - our directional label is TEXT-ONLY; no forward return ever enters any label, whereas hand-curating 'high-impact' events implicitly conditions on observed outcomes. (3) FORWARD-VOLATILITY PREDICTION BEYOND TRAILING VOL - our RQ1 (1-4h forward realized vol controlling for trailing-24h vol, vs an era + vol-quintile matched bootstrap null) has NO analog; Krause's vol discussion (Tariffs paper) is descriptive and does not net out vol clustering. (4) OUT-OF-SAMPLE TRADEABLE-EDGE RIGOR - we use a final-20%-by-time OOS holdout, 0.24% round-trip costs, block-bootstrap 95% Sharpe-CI lower bound, purged + 24h-embargoed walk-forward LightGBM CV, and Benjamini-Hochberg FDR + Romano-Wolf control; every Krause paper reports IN-SAMPLE CARs/t-tests with raw p-values, no cost adjustment, no OOS split, no multiple-testing correction. Frequency (our hourly vs their daily) is a real but SECONDARY difference and must be stated carefully because 'Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds' is itself billed 'high-frequency'.

## Cryptocurrency reactions to influential individuals' social media (Elon Musk -> crypto abnormal returns; influencer/celebrity signalling) and Truth Social as a research data source / political-communication dataset.

- **Lennart Ante (2023), "How Elon Musk's Twitter activity moves cryptocurrency markets," Technological Forecasting and Social Change, vol. 186, 122112 (working-paper version: Blockchain Research Lab WP No. 16; SSRN 3778844 / 4065313).**
  - Finding: Event study of 47 Musk crypto-related Twitter events finds significant positive abnormal returns and abnormal trading volume after events. Price effects are significant on average only for Dogecoin, not Bitcoin (positive and negative Bitcoin tweets roughly cancel); reported per-tweet Bitcoin moves of about +16.9% or -11.8%, and e.g. a Dogecoin abnormal return peaking ~17% within an hour. Volume effects significant for all events studied.
  - Relevance: This is the canonical 'influential individual moves crypto' precedent and the closest methodological cousin to RQ1/RQ2 (intraday/short-horizon abnormal returns on major coins). DIFFERS sharply: (a) hand-selected n=47 events vs our full uncurated ~33k-post corpus classified into a text-only signal; (b) Musk's posts are explicitly crypto-targeting, whereas Trump posts are mostly macro/policy and only ~1,851 are market-relevant; (c) Ante stops at abnormal returns/volume — no forward-realized-vol-beyond-trailing-vol test (our RQ1), no net-of-cost tradeable rule with bootstrap Sharpe CI (our RQ3), and no pre-registered multiple-testing control. Establishes the phenomenon; we test predictability and tradeability under look-ahead-free discipline.
- **David Krause (2025), "Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds: An Empirical Study of Digital Asset Reactions to Presidential Crypto Advocacy," SSRN Working Paper 5241126 (May 2025).**
  - Finding: Dual design (one-year return/risk evaluation + high-frequency event study) on hand-curated Trump-family crypto-advocacy / policy-endorsement events. Finds Bitcoin earns statistically significant excess returns in event windows tied to crypto-policy announcements, while Ethereum shows no comparable response and gold is stable; argues Bitcoin price is more tied to Trump political favorability than to traditional macro metrics.
  - Relevance: This is the explicit prior work named in our paper ('Krause, SSRN, daily event studies on HAND-CURATED policy events'). DIFFERS: curated discrete events vs our systematic classification of the entire Truth Social corpus into a continuous text-only signal (FinBERT + gpt-4.1-mini stance/conviction + frozen topic taxonomy + causal novelty); Krause does not run hourly forward realized vol vs trailing-24h (RQ1), a cost-netted hold-4h OOS rule (RQ3), or purged/embargoed CV with FDR + Romano-Wolf. Our burst-level, no-forward-returns-in-labels framing directly addresses the look-ahead/curation discretion latent in event-curation designs.
- **David Krause (2025), "The Trump Effect: An Event Study of Asymmetric Bitcoin Market Responses to Political News," SSRN Working Paper 5798822 (Nov 2025).**
  - Finding: Three-stage design (correlation, factor regression, market-adjusted event study) on daily data Jan 2024-Nov 2025. Trump approval changes have essentially no continuous predictive power over Bitcoin (r=.04); Bitcoin behaves as a high-beta equity proxy (beta=0.97). Across ~30 curated political/macro events the average abnormal return is significant only at 10% (p=.079); 'Political Symbolic' events strongest (4.49%, p=.059), then Regulatory Relief (2.21%), Market Access (2.51%), Executive Actions (0.81%).
  - Relevance: Most direct competitor: same actor (Trump), same primary asset (Bitcoin), event-study framing. Reinforces the SINGLE-FACTOR caveat our paper already flags (Bitcoin as high-beta risk asset) and the difficulty of finding continuous predictability. DIFFERS: ~30 hand-picked daily events vs full-corpus hourly signal; no realized-vol RQ, no tradeable-edge RQ, no multiple-testing correction. Their weak/marginal daily significance is exactly why our hourly vol effect (RQ1) and look-ahead-free signal construction add value rather than duplicate.

## High-frequency / intraday event-study methodology for 24/7 crypto markets: abnormal returns, realized volatility estimation, jumps, and why intraday resolution matters vs daily CARs.

- **Krause, David (2025). "The Trump Effect: An Event Study of Asymmetric Bitcoin Market Responses to Political News." SSRN Working Paper, abstract_id=5798822 (Nov 24, 2025). See also Krause (2025), "Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds," SSRN abstract_id=5241126, and Krause (2025), "Trump-Era Cryptocurrency Policy Events and Bitcoin Returns," SSRN abstract_id=5863983.**
  - Finding: Event studies of HAND-CURATED Trump/administration political and crypto-policy events on Bitcoin. Finds asymmetric and category-dependent abnormal returns: e.g., 'Political Symbolic' events ~+4.49% (p=0.059), 'Regulatory Relief' ~+2.21% (p=0.020), 'Market Access' ~+2.51%, 'Executive Actions' ~+0.81%; Bitcoin markets both respond to general regulatory-favorability signaling and distinguish between administrative mechanisms.
  - Relevance: This is the explicitly named baseline/foil for the study under review. Krause uses DAILY (or low-frequency) CARs over a small set of analyst-selected events. Our study differs on three axes: (1) we use the FULL ~33k-post corpus with a text-only, look-ahead-free classifier rather than hand-picking events (removing selection/circularity bias); (2) we measure response at HOURLY/1-4h resolution where Krause's daily window would dilute or miss the effect (the 2025-04-09 pause came ~4h after the post); (3) we add a trailing-vol-matched null and purged/embargoed CV that daily-CAR designs do not employ.
- **Nishimura, Yusaku; Dong, Xuyi; Sun, Bianxia (2021). "Trump's tweets: Sentiment, stock market volatility, and jumps." Journal of Financial Research 44(3): 497-512. https://doi.org/10.1111/jfir.12248**
  - Finding: Decomposes high-frequency realized volatility of US equity indices into continuous and jump components and links them to tweet sentiment. Finds Trump's tweeting positively and significantly raises both realized volatility and jumps; the RV response exceeds the continuous-volatility response precisely BECAUSE of the jump component, and tweets raise jump tail risk that cannot be hedged without paying extra premium.
  - Relevance: The single closest METHODOLOGICAL precedent: a political-communication signal mapped onto intraday realized-volatility-plus-jump decomposition. Validates our RQ1 framing (text signal -> forward RV) and the relevance of separating jumps. Differs in that it studies session-market EQUITIES (overnight gaps, no 24/7 trading), tests a sentiment construct without explicit look-ahead controls, and does not test a tradeable net-of-cost edge (our RQ3). Our study ports this template to 24/7 crypto with text-only labels and circularity/embargo controls.
- **Yang, Manlu; Wang, Yufeng (2026). "Scheduled FOMC statements and intraday macro event risk in cryptocurrency markets." Finance Research Letters 101(C), 110073. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.frl.2026.110073**
  - Finding: Using 41 scheduled FOMC statement releases (Jan 2021-Jan 2026) and HOURLY BTC-USD / ETH-USD candles, finds mean absolute returns in the first post-statement hour (14:00-15:00 ET) rise from 0.66% to 1.25% (BTC) and 0.85% to 1.50% (ETH), with significant USD-volume increases; crypto embeds sizeable scheduled macro event risk at communication times.
  - Relevance: Near-identical EMPIRICAL SETUP to RQ1 (hourly BTC/ETH bars, intraday event window, vol/return-magnitude response), and a peer-reviewed anchor for the FOMC effect. Key difference: their events are SCHEDULED and exogenous (known timestamp, calendar-fixed), so no signal-extraction or look-ahead problem arises. Our events are STOCHASTICALLY TIMED, text-derived bursts that must be classified without forward returns -- the harder identification problem they sidestep. We also add SOL, a daily macro panel, and a trailing-24h-vol-matched null to separate the signal from generic vol clustering.
- **Andersen, Torben G.; Bollerslev, Tim; Diebold, Francis X.; Vega, Clara (2003). "Micro Effects of Macro Announcements: Real-Time Price Discovery in Foreign Exchange." American Economic Review 93(1): 38-62.**
  - Finding: Establishes the canonical high-frequency event-study template: regress intraday (5-min) returns and volatility on the SURPRISE component of scheduled macro announcements, showing announcements drive conditional mean jumps and persistent volatility responses, with asymmetric (sign-dependent) reactions.
  - Relevance: The methodological ancestor of RQ1/RQ2 -- intraday returns/volatility responding to an information arrival at a known timestamp, with a continuous-time mean+volatility decomposition. Our study generalizes 'announcement surprise' to a multi-component TEXT signal (FinBERT + LLM stance/conviction + topic + novelty) and applies it to 24/7 crypto rather than FX during business hours. The 'surprise' analog in our design is the causal 7-day novelty term.
- **Barndorff-Nielsen, Ole E.; Shephard, Neil (2004). "Power and Bipower Variation with Stochastic Volatility and Jumps." Journal of Financial Econometrics 2(1): 1-37. (Jump TEST: Barndorff-Nielsen & Shephard (2006), "Econometrics of Testing for Jumps in Financial Economics Using Bipower Variation," J. Financial Econometrics 4(1):1-30.)**
  - Finding: Introduces (realized) bipower variation, which is robust to rare jumps; the difference between realized variance and bipower variation consistently estimates the JUMP component of quadratic variation, enabling a formal test for the presence of jumps in high-frequency price paths.
  - Relevance: Provides the estimator/test machinery for separating continuous volatility from jumps in realized-volatility event studies. Relevant because RQ1's forward-RV effect (~+5bp/4h) may be partly jump-driven; this is the standard tool to decompose it. Our study uses realized volatility on hourly bars; BNS is the reference for any continuous-vs-jump split and a robustness check the related-work section should position against (alongside Lee-Mykland for jump TIMING).
- **Corsi, Fulvio (2009). "A Simple Approximate Long-Memory Model of Realized Volatility." Journal of Financial Econometrics 7(2): 174-196.**
  - Finding: Introduces the Heterogeneous Autoregressive (HAR-RV) model: realized volatility is well predicted by an additive cascade of its own past realizations over daily/weekly/monthly horizons, reproducing long memory, fat tails, and self-similarity in a parsimonious AR-type specification.
  - Relevance: Directly underpins RQ1's design choice to test forward RV BEYOND trailing-24h vol. HAR-RV is exactly why RV is strongly forecastable from its own recent history -- so an unconditioned 'bursts predict vol' finding is confounded by vol clustering. The study's trailing-vol control and trailing-vol-quintile-matched null are the appropriate way to net out the HAR-type baseline; this paper is the citation for why that control is necessary (the Phase-0 24h effect was vol clustering).
- **Lee, Suzanne S.; Mykland, Per A. (2008). "Jumps in Financial Markets: A New Nonparametric Test and Jump Dynamics." Review of Financial Studies 21(6): 2535-2563.**
  - Finding: A nonparametric test that identifies the EXACT INTRADAY TIMING and size of jumps by standardizing each intraday return by a local (robust) volatility estimate; finds individual-stock jumps cluster around scheduled firm news and index jumps around general market-news announcements.
  - Relevance: Methodologically central to 'why intraday resolution matters': it can attribute a specific jump to a specific timestamped information event -- something daily CARs cannot do. Supports anchoring our event window to the first post's hour and testing whether bursts coincide with detectable jumps. Differs in that Lee-Mykland is a price-process test, not an event classifier; our contribution is feeding a text-derived, stochastically-timed signal into this intraday-resolution frame on 24/7 assets.
- **Brattle Group (2023). "How Event Studies Can Be Applied to Crypto Markets." White paper. (See also Aharon et al. / Almeida & Goncalves (2024), "Application of Event Study Methodology in the Analysis of Cryptocurrency Returns," Emerging Markets Finance and Trade 61(4), https://doi.org/10.1080/1540496X.2024.2404173.)**
  - Finding: Documents the structural problems of running event studies in 24/7 crypto: no predetermined open/close, no overnight gap, continuous trading split into Asian/European/US activity phases, and the consequent need to redefine estimation/event windows (e.g., 365-day rolling windows rather than the 252-day equity convention).
  - Relevance: Justifies the study's departure from equity event-study conventions: in 24/7 crypto there is no 'event date' close-to-close return, which is precisely why the study uses HOURLY bursts anchored to the first post's hour and 1/4/24h forward windows rather than daily CARs. This is the explicit methodological warrant for intraday-over-daily resolution that distinguishes our design from Krause's daily-CAR approach.

**Gap we fill:** Intraday realized-volatility and jump methodology was developed for SESSION markets (FX/equities) and applied almost exclusively to SCHEDULED or HAND-CURATED information events: ABDL-Vega (2003) and Lee-Mykland (2008) use timestamped scheduled announcements; Nishimura et al. (2021) port this to Trump tweets but on session-market equities with overnight gaps and no look-ahead controls; Yang & Wang (2026) hit hourly BTC/ETH but only for the 41 calendar-fixed FOMC releases; and the crypto event-study work that uses the FULL signal universe (Krause) reverts to DAILY CARs over analyst-selected events, which dilutes or misses the 1-4h window where the effect actually lives. No existing paper in this slice tests a STOCHASTICALLY-TIMED, TEXT-DERIVED, strictly look-ahead-free signal (built from the full ~33k-post corpus, never from forward returns) at HOURLY resolution on 24/7 crypto, with (a) a null matched on era AND trailing-24h-vol quintile to net out HAR-type vol clustering (Corsi 2009), (b) purged + 24h-embargoed walk-forward CV, and (c) a pre-registered net-of-cost tradeable-edge test judged by a block-bootstrap Sharpe CI lower bound (Ledoit-Wolf style). That combination -- full-corpus text signal + intraday crypto resolution + circularity/embargo discipline + vol-matched null + Sharpe-CI edge test -- is the contribution relative to this slice.

## The econometrics of volatility clustering and endogenous event/news timing: GARCH, the news-arrival-vs-volatility literature, and methods to separate "the announcement caused the volatility" from "the announcement arrived because volatility was already high." This is the study's core identification concern (RQ1: does a market-relevant burst predict 1-4h forward realized vol BEYOND trailing-24h vol).

- **Engle, R.F. (1982). Autoregressive Conditional Heteroscedasticity with Estimates of the Variance of United Kingdom Inflation. Econometrica 50(4):987-1008.**
  - Finding: Introduces ARCH: conditional variance is a deterministic function of recent squared innovations, formalizing volatility clustering (large moves follow large moves). Verified via Econometrica / Sewell mirror.
  - Relevance: This is the formal definition of the confound the study must defeat. The whole point of RQ1's 'beyond trailing-24h vol' control is that clustering means an elevated vol reading after a burst could simply be ARCH persistence, not a causal news effect. The study's matched null (era + trailing-vol quintile) is an attempt to net out exactly the Engle-style autocorrelation in variance.
- **Bollerslev, T. (1986). Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity. Journal of Econometrics 31(3):307-327.**
  - Finding: Generalizes ARCH to GARCH(p,q) by letting today's conditional variance depend on its own past values as well as past squared shocks, capturing the long, slowly-decaying persistence in financial volatility with few parameters. Verified via Duke/Bollerslev published-papers mirror and ScienceDirect.
  - Relevance: GARCH persistence is precisely why a 24h window is contaminated: a burst that lands during a high-variance regime will be mechanically followed by high variance for hours regardless of its content. The study's own honest decomposition ('the 24h effect WAS vol clustering; only the 1-4h residual survives') is a GARCH statement -- it is conceding that the longer-horizon signal is the GARCH/Bollerslev memory term, not causation.
- **Clark, P.K. (1973). A Subordinated Stochastic Process Model with Finite Variance for Speculative Prices. Econometrica 41(1):135-155.**
  - Finding: Origin of the Mixture-of-Distributions Hypothesis (MDH): price changes are normal conditional on a latent, time-varying information/activity rate (a directing process), so observed fat tails and clustering arise from variation in the news-arrival rate itself. Verified via Econometric Society and multiple reference mirrors.
  - Relevance: MDH is the theoretical reason news arrival and volatility are co-determined by a common latent driver -- the deepest form of the study's 'announcement arrives when vol is already high' problem. If bursts and volatility are both subordinate to one information-flow clock, a naive event study attributes to the burst what the clock generated. The study's text-only signal (no forward returns ever) is an attempt to get a burst measure that is NOT itself a function of the realized activity rate.
- **Tauchen, G.E. & Pitts, M. (1983). The Price Variability-Volume Relationship on Speculative Markets. Econometrica 51(2):485-505.**
  - Finding: Formalizes MDH empirically: the joint distribution of daily price change and trading volume is governed by the number of within-day information arrivals; both volatility and volume rise with the arrival rate. Verified via Semantic Scholar / Econometrica references.
  - Relevance: Establishes that volume (and by extension any activity/engagement count) is endogenous to the same latent arrival rate as volatility. This is the formal justification for the study's design choice to EXCLUDE engagement counts from predictors (also because they are not known at post time) -- using them would re-introduce the MDH simultaneity the study is trying to avoid.
- **Andersen, T.G. (1996). Return Volatility and Trading Volume: An Information Flow Interpretation of Stochastic Volatility. Journal of Finance 51(1):169-204.**
  - Finding: Builds a microstructure-founded modified MDH where a stochastic-volatility information-flow process drives both returns and volume, and shows it generalizes/outperforms standard ARCH and standard MDH; explicitly frames volatility clustering as the footprint of serially-correlated information flow. Verified via Wiley/JF and Kellogg listing.
  - Relevance: Bridges MDH and GARCH: clustering IS persistent information flow. For the study this means the trailing-24h-vol control is a proxy for the latent flow state, and a burst's apparent predictive power is suspect unless it survives conditioning on that state -- which is exactly what the matched-quintile null does.
- **Andersen, T.G. & Bollerslev, T. (1998). Deutsche Mark-Dollar Volatility: Intraday Activity Patterns, Macroeconomic Announcements, and Longer Run Dependencies. Journal of Finance 53(1):219-265.**
  - Finding: Using 5-min DM/USD returns, scheduled macro announcements produce sharp, identifiable volatility responses, but their share of total volatility is small relative to intraday periodicity and to standard (clustering-driven) volatility forecasts at the daily level. Verified via Duke/Bollerslev mirror and Wiley.
  - Relevance: The canonical high-frequency announcement-vs-volatility benchmark and a cautionary result: even cleanly-timed, exogenous, SCHEDULED news explains only a modest slice of volatility once periodicity and persistence are accounted for. It rationalizes the study's small, honest 1-4h effect (~+5bp/4h) and its insistence on controlling trailing vol -- a large raw post-burst vol number would mostly be clustering, not news.
- **Lamoureux, C.G. & Lastrapes, W.D. (1990). Heteroskedasticity in Stock Return Data: Volume versus GARCH Effects. Journal of Finance 45(1):221-229.**
  - Finding: When daily trading volume (a proxy for information arrival) is added to the GARCH variance equation, the GARCH persistence terms collapse toward insignificance -- i.e., an information-arrival proxy can 'explain away' apparent volatility clustering. They also flag the simultaneity/endogeneity of using a contemporaneous proxy. Verified via Wiley/JF and RePEc.
  - Relevance: The single tightest methodological match to RQ1. RQ1 ('does a burst predict forward vol BEYOND trailing-24h vol') is structurally the Lamoureux-Lastrapes test run in reverse: instead of seeing whether a proxy kills GARCH, the study asks whether a news proxy adds anything AFTER the GARCH/trailing-vol term. Their simultaneity critique (the proxy is contemporaneous with vol) is the explicit reason the study went text-only with NO forward returns in any label -- to obtain a news measure that is predetermined rather than jointly determined with the volatility it is meant to explain.
- **Lee, S.S. & Mykland, P.A. (2008). Jumps in Financial Markets: A New Nonparametric Test and Jump Dynamics. Review of Financial Studies 21(6):2535-2563.**
  - Finding: Nonparametric test that flags a return as a jump when it is large relative to local (jump-robust bipower) volatility; finds individual-stock jumps cluster at prescheduled earnings/company news and index jumps at general macro news. Verified via Oxford/RFS, JSTOR, RePEc.
  - Relevance: Methodologically important because it normalizes candidate events by LOCAL volatility before calling them significant -- the same logic as the study's trailing-vol control, but at the jump level. It also documents the scheduled-vs-unscheduled distinction: the study's Truth Social bursts are UNSCHEDULED (timing unknown ex ante), which removes the pre-announcement window confound of scheduled macro releases but introduces the self-selection-of-timing confound this slice targets.
- **Acharya, V.V., DeMarzo, P. & Kremer, I. (2011). Endogenous Information Flows and the Clustering of Announcements. American Economic Review 101(7):2955-2979. (Working-paper versions: NBER w16485; SSRN 1275133.)**
  - Finding: A strategic-disclosure model where firms time their announcements relative to aggregate news: bad economy-wide news triggers immediate voluntary disclosure while good news delays it, endogenously producing clustering of (especially negative) announcements and affecting conditional variance/skewness of returns. Verified via AEA listing and NBER.
  - Relevance: The strongest theoretical anchor for 'announcements arrive when volatility/conditions are already adverse.' It says the timing of disclosure is itself a strategic response to the state of the market, so announcement clustering is a SELECTION outcome, not exogenous. Directly analogous to a politician posting market-relevant messages WHEN markets are already turbulent (e.g., the 2025 tariff-war regime the study notes dominates its sample) -- which is the reverse-causality story the study must rule out, and motivates matching the null on era.
- **Rambaldi, M., Pennesi, P. & Lillo, F. (2014). Modeling FX market activity around macroeconomic news: a Hawkes process approach. arXiv:1405.6047 (q-fin).**
  - Finding: A Hawkes (self-exciting) point-process model decomposes FX activity into an endogenous self-exciting component (events beget events) and an exogenous component tied to pre-announced macro news; captures the activity surge after news even when the news has negligible volatility impact, and adds non-causal pre-announcement effects. Verified via arXiv abstract.
  - Relevance: Formalizes the arrival-timing confound the slice is about: self-excitation means a cluster of events can be endogenously generated by prior events/volatility, not by fresh exogenous information. The study's unit of analysis -- the 'burst' (posts <30min apart) -- is itself a self-exciting cluster, so Hawkes is the natural model for WHY bursts cluster in time and why a burst landing inside an active regime needs the era + trailing-vol-quintile null to avoid crediting endogenous self-excitation as a causal news effect.
- **Rigobon, R. (2003). Identification Through Heteroskedasticity. Review of Economics and Statistics 85(4):777-792. (Companion application: Rigobon & Sack (2003), Measuring the Reaction of Monetary Policy to the Stock Market, Quarterly Journal of Economics 118(2):639-669.)**
  - Finding: Solves simultaneous-equations identification by exploiting shifts in the variance of structural shocks across regimes -- when an event and the outcome are jointly determined, regime-dependent heteroskedasticity provides the moments needed to separate cause from effect without a clean instrument. Verified via REStat/RePEc and QJE listings.
  - Relevance: A direct method for the slice's core problem when 'the announcement and the volatility are simultaneously determined.' It is the formal alternative to the study's matched-null approach: rather than matching on trailing-vol quintile, identification-through-heteroskedasticity uses the change in variance itself to break simultaneity. Worth citing as the econometric ideal the study approximates with a (more robust, distribution-free) bootstrap null and purged/embargoed walk-forward CV when no discrete bracketable event and clean instrument exist.
- **Casini, A. & McCloskey, A. (2024). Identification and Estimation of Causal Effects in High-Frequency Event Studies. arXiv:2406.15667 (econ.EM).**
  - Finding: Proves that a narrow event window is NOT sufficient for causal identification in high-frequency event studies; valid inference additionally requires (i) separability (the shock's effect does not interact with other variables in the window) and (ii) relative exogeneity (the surprise's variance must dominate all confounding variation in the window). Verified via arXiv abstract.
  - Relevance: The modern statement that 'just put a tight window around the announcement' does not buy clean causal identification -- which is exactly the trap a burst-level event study could fall into. It justifies why the study does NOT rely on window narrowness alone but layers an era + trailing-vol-quintile matched null, day-clustered OLS with a trailing-vol control, FDR/Romano-Wolf multiplicity control, and purged+embargoed CV. The study's confound (vol already high inside the window) is a violation of their relative-exogeneity condition.
- **Krause, D. (2025). Trump-Era Cryptocurrency Policy Events and Bitcoin Returns: An Event Study Analysis. SSRN Working Paper 5863983. (Related: Krause, The Trump Effect: An Event Study of Asymmetric Bitcoin Market Responses to Political News, SSRN 5798822; Trump, Tokens, and Tailwinds, SSRN 5241126.)**
  - Finding: DAILY event study on HAND-SELECTED Trump-era crypto/policy events: reports a significant collective ~2.2% 3-day cumulative abnormal return with category differences (political-symbolic strongest). Verified via SSRN listings; effect magnitudes are the SSRN summary's, treat as indicative.
  - Relevance: The closest prior work and the explicit foil the study names. Krause uses (a) DAILY bars, (b) a SMALL HAND-CURATED set of events chosen with knowledge of their salience, and (c) standard CAR event-study mechanics with no formal control for the endogenous-timing / clustering confound. The study under review differs on every identification-relevant axis: the FULL ~33k-post corpus (no hand-selection), HOURLY crypto bars, a pre-registered text-only signal with no forward-return leakage, and a matched null + purged CV specifically built to separate causation from clustering. Krause sits in the application slice; it does not address this slice's econometric identification question.

**Gap we fill:** This slice's literature splits three ways and none of the strands closes the loop for this study's setting. (1) The MDH/GARCH spine (Clark 1973; Tauchen-Pitts 1983; Engle 1982; Bollerslev 1986; Andersen 1996; Andersen-Bollerslev 1998; Lamoureux-Lastrapes 1990) establishes that news arrival and volatility are co-determined by a latent information-flow process and that volatility clusters/persists -- it DEFINES the confound but, where it nets news out at all (Lamoureux-Lastrapes), it uses a contemporaneous, endogenous volume proxy on daily equities, not a predetermined, text-only news measure. (2) The endogenous-timing strand (Acharya-DeMarzo-Kremer 2011; Rambaldi-Pennesi-Lillo 2014) models WHY announcements cluster when conditions are adverse (strategic disclosure) or self-excite (Hawkes), but does so THEORETICALLY and for firm disclosures / scheduled macro releases with known timing -- not for an unscheduled, self-exciting, social-media source. (3) The identification-method strand (Rigobon 2003; Casini-McCloskey 2024; Lee-Mykland 2008) provides clean tools, but each assumes either a discrete bracketable event with regime-shifted variance, or a tight window with relative exogeneity, or a jump normalized by local vol -- conditions a stream of ~33k overlapping social-media posts in one tariff-war regime does not meet. The study fills the gap by handling an UNSCHEDULED, SELF-EXCITING, SOCIAL-MEDIA news source on HOURLY crypto with a PRE-REGISTERED, TEXT-ONLY (no-forward-return) burst-level design that explicitly separates causation from clustering via (a) a bootstrap null matched on era + trailing-24h-vol quintile, (b) day-clustered OLS with a trailing-vol control, and (c) purged + embargoed walk-forward CV with FDR + Romano-Wolf multiplicity control. Its key contribution to THIS slice is the honest decomposition that the apparent 24h effect WAS volatility clustering (the GARCH/MDH memory term) while only a small 1-4h residual survives the matched null -- i.e., it answers the slice's question ('did the announcement cause it, or did it just arrive when vol was high?') on its own data and largely attributes the longer-horizon signal to the confound rather than to the news.

## Multiple-testing and backtest-overfitting controls in empirical finance (HLZ; Harvey-Liu Lucky Factors; Lopez de Prado purged/embargoed CV and Deflated Sharpe Ratio; Bailey et al. backtest overfitting / PBO; Romano-Wolf stepdown), with an assessment of whether BH-FDR + Romano-Wolf is the right/defensible control pair for the Trump-Truth-Social signal study.

- **Harvey, C. R., Liu, Y., & Zhu, H. (2016). '...and the Cross-Section of Expected Returns.' Review of Financial Studies 29(1): 5-68. (NBER WP 20592; SSRN 2249314).**
  - Finding: Catalogs 300+ published return factors and argues most are likely false discoveries from collective data mining. Proposes a multiple-testing framework (Bonferroni, Holm, BHY) that accommodates correlation among tests and missing data, and concludes a newly proposed factor should clear roughly a t-stat > 3.0 hurdle rather than the conventional 2.0.
  - Relevance: Canonical statement of the multiplicity problem in finance and the source of the 'raise the bar' logic the study adopts. KEY: HLZ themselves use Benjamini-Hochberg-Yekutieli FDR as one of their three recommended corrections, which directly legitimizes the study's BH-FDR primary control. DIFFERS: HLZ addresses factor-zoo multiplicity (hundreds of competing predictors across the whole literature); the study has a small, pre-registered test grid (3 RQs x a handful of assets/horizons), so the t>3 hurdle calibrated for ~300 factors is not the relevant cutoff here. The study's real overfitting temptation is taxonomy-snooping, not test-count explosion - which is why it should cite HLZ for the principle but not import its specific hurdle.
- **Harvey, C. R., & Liu, Y. (2021). 'Lucky Factors.' Journal of Financial Economics 141(2): 413-435. (SSRN 2528780).**
  - Finding: A bootstrap method that, by imposing the null of no cross-sectional explanatory power and resampling, controls the multiple-testing problem while sequentially adding factors; tests on individual stocks rather than arbitrary portfolio sorts to avoid power loss. The bootstrap naturally captures cross-correlation among candidate test statistics.
  - Relevance: Demonstrates the now-standard practice of using a dependence-aware BOOTSTRAP to control multiplicity rather than analytic Bonferroni-type bounds. This is the same family of logic as the study's Romano-Wolf step and its era+vol-quintile-matched bootstrap null. DIFFERS: Lucky Factors is a model-selection/factor-addition engine for a large candidate pool; the study uses bootstrap matching for a single, pre-specified event-study contrast. Cite as precedent for 'bootstrap-based, correlation-respecting multiplicity control is the accepted modern tool.'
- **Romano, J. P., & Wolf, M. (2005). 'Stepwise Multiple Testing as Formalized Data Snooping.' Econometrica 73(4): 1237-1282. (SSRN 563209). Implementation: Clarke, Romano & Wolf (2020), Stata Journal 20(4).**
  - Finding: A stepdown procedure that asymptotically controls the familywise error rate (FWER) and is uniformly more powerful than Bonferroni/Holm because it bootstraps the JOINT dependence structure of the test statistics rather than assuming independence.
  - Relevance: This is the load-bearing justification for the study's RW step. The study's BTC/ETH/SOL co-move at ~0.92 (effectively one factor), so the test statistics across crypto assets are highly positively dependent; Bonferroni/Holm would be badly over-conservative, whereas Romano-Wolf's resampling of the joint null is exactly designed for this case and recovers power. RW is also the canonical FWER answer to the trading-rule data-snooping problem, making it the right strict-control partner for RQ3's sign-rule test. The choice is well-matched, not arbitrary.
- **Benjamini, Y., & Hochberg, Y. (1995). 'Controlling the False Discovery Rate: A Practical and Powerful Approach to Multiple Testing.' JRSS-B 57(1): 289-300. AND Benjamini, Y., & Yekutieli, D. (2001). 'The Control of the False Discovery Rate in Multiple Testing under Dependency.' Annals of Statistics 29(4): 1165-1188.**
  - Finding: BH (1995) controls the expected false discovery proportion (FDR) and is more powerful than FWER methods when many hypotheses are tested; valid under independence or positive regression dependency (PRDS). BY (2001) extends FDR control to ARBITRARY dependence via a log-factor penalty, at a cost in power.
  - Relevance: Anchors the study's BH-FDR q=0.10 primary control. CAVEAT the study must state: vanilla BH requires independence/PRDS. The strong POSITIVE cross-asset correlation (0.92) is plausibly PRDS-consistent, so BH likely retains FDR control here - but the robust fallback under arbitrary dependence is Benjamini-Yekutieli, which a referee will demand be named. Reporting BH-FDR (lenient) alongside Romano-Wolf FWER (strict) is a defensible strict+lenient bracket, not hedging.
- **Bailey, D. H., & Lopez de Prado, M. (2014). 'The Deflated Sharpe Ratio: Correcting for Selection Bias, Backtest Overfitting, and Non-Normality.' Journal of Portfolio Management 40(5): 94-107. (SSRN 2460551).**
  - Finding: The DSR deflates an observed Sharpe ratio for (a) the number of independent strategy configurations tried and (b) non-normal (skewed/fat-tailed) returns, returning the probability that the true Sharpe exceeds zero given the selection process. A block-bootstrap CI corrects sampling error but NOT selection-across-trials inflation.
  - Relevance: Directly bears on RQ3, which judges a tradeable edge via a block-bootstrap 95% Sharpe-CI lower bound. ASSESSMENT: the study's rule (sign(signal), 4h hold, 0.24% cost) is pre-registered as a single configuration, so N_trials is approximately 1 and DSR's deflation term is near-zero - in that case the block-bootstrap CI is sufficient and DSR adds little. The study should state this explicitly. IF any of hold-length, cost threshold, or asset set were tuned, the block-bootstrap CI alone is insufficient and DSR (or PBO) should be reported. Crypto returns are fat-tailed, so DSR's non-normality correction is a free robustness add even at N_trials=1.
- **Bailey, D. H., Borwein, J. M., Lopez de Prado, M., & Zhu, Q. J. (2014). 'Pseudo-Mathematics and Financial Charlatanism: The Effects of Backtest Overfitting on Out-of-Sample Performance.' Notices of the AMS 61(5): 458-471 (SSRN 2308659); and Bailey, Borwein, Lopez de Prado & Zhu (2017), 'The Probability of Backtest Overfitting,' Journal of Computational Finance 20(4): 39-69 (SSRN 2326253).**
  - Finding: Prove that high in-sample Sharpe is trivially achievable by trying a modest number of configurations even on pure noise, and that OOS performance degrades (often goes negative) with the number tried. Introduce PBO via Combinatorially-Symmetric Cross-Validation (CSCV) to estimate the probability a selected backtest is overfit, and the minimum-backtest-length idea.
  - Relevance: Establishes the principle that the number of configurations tried is the key overfitting driver - the reason the study's pre-registration (methods fixed BEFORE results) and its explicit Phase-0/confirmatory split are the right structural defenses. DIFFERS: CSCV/PBO targets a large grid of candidate strategy configurations; the study runs essentially one pre-specified rule per RQ. So PBO machinery is largely unnecessary IF pre-registration held - cite to show the study addresses the root cause (trial count) by design rather than by post-hoc correction.
- **Lopez de Prado, M. (2018). Advances in Financial Machine Learning, Wiley (Ch. 7: Cross-Validation in Finance). See also 'The 10 Reasons Most Machine Learning Funds Fail.'**
  - Finding: Standard k-fold CV leaks information in financial data because labels overlap across the train/test boundary, inflating apparent skill. Introduces PURGING (drop training samples whose label windows overlap the test fold) and EMBARGOING (a buffer after each test fold) to restore chronological integrity, and Combinatorial Purged CV for robust performance distributions.
  - Relevance: Direct methodological precedent for the study's LightGBM with PURGED + EMBARGOED (24h) walk-forward CV. The 24h embargo is exactly the LdP prescription, sized to the 1-4h forward-return label horizon plus the burst structure, so leakage from overlapping forward-return windows is controlled. This is the correct ML-leakage control and is implemented as specified; no gap here other than confirming the embargo >= max label horizon (4h) which 24h comfortably satisfies.
- **Harvey, C. R. (2017). 'Presidential Address: The Scientific Outlook in Financial Economics.' Journal of Finance 72(4): 1399-1440. (SSRN 2893930).**
  - Finding: Argues empirical finance over-relies on p-values; unreported tests, failure to adjust for multiplicity, and direct/indirect p-hacking produce many false positives that fail OOS. Advocates pre-registration, higher evidentiary bars, data/code sharing, and Bayesianized p-values (minimum Bayes factor x prior odds).
  - Relevance: The canonical finance citation for the study's central defensive posture: PRE-REGISTRATION of methods before results, the no-look-ahead text-only labels, and the hypothesis-generating vs confirmatory (Phase-0) separation. Anchors why the study's design choices are the recommended remedy rather than ad hoc. Use to motivate the whole controls section.
- **Supporting trading-rule data-snooping lineage: White, H. (2000), 'A Reality Check for Data Snooping,' Econometrica 68(5): 1097-1126; Sullivan, Timmermann & White (1999), 'Data-Snooping, Technical Trading Rule Performance, and the Bootstrap,' Journal of Finance 54(5): 1647-1691; Hansen, P. R. (2005), 'A Test for Superior Predictive Ability,' JBES 23(4): 365-380. Plus Barras, Scaillet & Wermers (2010), 'False Discoveries in Mutual Fund Performance,' Journal of Finance 65(1): 179-216 (FDR applied to fund alphas).**
  - Finding: White's Reality Check and Hansen's SPA test the best rule against a benchmark accounting for the full universe of rules searched (bootstrap, dependence-aware); STW apply this to thousands of technical trading rules and find apparent profits largely vanish. Barras-Scaillet-Wermers apply FDR to ~2,000 mutual funds, finding ~75% zero-alpha and almost no skilled funds by 2006.
  - Relevance: Situates the study's RQ3 in the trading-rule data-snooping tradition and shows FDR has an established finance pedigree (BSW) beyond the factor zoo. Romano-Wolf is the stepdown refinement of White/Hansen, so the study's RW choice sits squarely in this lineage. These are context/lineage citations rather than methods the study must add.

**Gap we fill:** This literature was built for two large-multiplicity regimes: the factor zoo (hundreds of competing return predictors - HLZ, Lucky Factors) and trading-rule universes (thousands of TA rules - White/STW/Hansen/Romano-Wolf), plus ML-leakage control (Lopez de Prado purged/embargoed CV) and Sharpe-inflation correction (Deflated Sharpe Ratio / PBO). The study imports this heavy artillery - dependence-aware FWER (Romano-Wolf) + FDR (BH) + purged/embargoed walk-forward CV - into a SMALL, PRE-REGISTERED event-study grid where the dominant overfitting risk is not test-count explosion but TAXONOMY-SNOOPING (refining the keyword/topic taxonomy after observing 2025 episodes), which the design isolates via an explicit Phase-0 hypothesis-generating vs confirmatory split. The specific contribution to this slice is methodological transfer plus a fitted control choice: (i) Romano-Wolf is the correct strict control precisely BECAUSE the three crypto assets co-move at ~0.92 (RW's bootstrap exploits that joint dependence where Bonferroni would be over-conservative); (ii) BH-FDR is the right lenient bracket, with the PRDS caveat (and Benjamini-Yekutieli as the arbitrary-dependence fallback) explicitly acknowledged; (iii) the no-forward-returns-in-labels rule and pre-registration address the trial-count root cause (Bailey et al., Harvey 2017) by design rather than post hoc. The honest boundary: none of these multiplicity controls fix the single-regime (2025-26 tariff-war) non-stationarity caveat - MT controls bound false positives from multiplicity, not from regime dependence - and RQ3's block-bootstrap Sharpe CI corrects sampling error but not selection-across-trials, so the study should state that its rule is single-config (DSR deflation approximately zero) or otherwise report a Deflated Sharpe Ratio.

## AI-augmented / LLM-assisted scientific research and meta-science: LLMs as research assistants, autonomous-vs-augmented research, reproducibility of LLM-in-the-loop pipelines, and how 'AI boost' contributions are framed/credited.

- **Ludwig, J., Mullainathan, S., & Rambachan, A. (2024). "Large Language Models: An Applied Econometric Framework." arXiv:2412.07031 [econ.EM] (NBER working paper draft). Verified via arXiv abstract page.**
  - Finding: Develops a formal econometric framework for two distinct uses of LLMs in empirical economics: (1) PREDICTION tasks, where the central threat is training-data leakage/look-ahead (the LLM may have 'seen' the outcome), and (2) MEASUREMENT-automation tasks, where the LLM labels text and those labels become economic variables in downstream inference. For the measurement case it shows valid inference requires a human-validated sample to bound classification error; absent that, downstream coefficients/standard errors are unreliable.
  - Relevance: This is the single most load-bearing cite. Our study is exactly their 'measurement automation' case: gpt-4.1-mini stance/conviction and FinBERT sentiment are LLM-produced labels fed into OLS and LightGBM. Their two named threats map directly onto our two core design choices: their 'leakage' threat = our text-only labels with NO forward returns ever entering a label (anti-look-ahead/circularity); their 'validation-sample' requirement is the discipline our pre-registration and FDR/Romano-Wolf grid embody. We differ by instantiating the framework concretely in a high-frequency crypto/macro setting with purged+embargoed walk-forward CV — the empirical demonstration their framework prescribes.
- **Gilardi, F., Alizadeh, M., & Kubli, M. (2023). "ChatGPT outperforms crowd workers for text-annotation tasks." Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS) 120(30): e2305016120. Also arXiv:2303.15056. Verified via PNAS DOI and arXiv.**
  - Finding: On 6,183 tweets/news articles across relevance, stance, topic, and frame-detection tasks, zero-shot ChatGPT beat MTurk crowd workers by ~25 percentage points in accuracy and exceeded both crowd and trained-annotator intercoder agreement, at ~1/30th the cost.
  - Relevance: Foundational justification for using an LLM as the annotation instrument in a research pipeline — precisely our use of gpt-4.1-mini for stance(-2..+2), conviction, is_policy_signal, is_market_directive on the 1,851 market-relevant posts, and FinBERT for sentiment. Notably the tasks they validate (stance, relevance, topic) are nearly identical to ours. We extend beyond their single-shot accuracy claim by treating these labels as inputs to a forward-looking financial prediction task and stress-testing for circularity, which their text-classification benchmark does not address.
- **Atil, B., Aykent, S., Chittams, A., Fu, L., Passonneau, R. J., Radcliffe, E., et al. (2024). "Non-Determinism of 'Deterministic' LLM Settings." arXiv:2408.04667 (rev. Apr 2025). Verified via arXiv abstract page.**
  - Finding: Even at temperature 0 / 'deterministic' settings, the same prompt yields different outputs across runs for all five LLMs tested, with accuracy varying up to ~15% and task-performance gaps up to 70%; introduces TARr@N / TARa@N metrics to quantify run-to-run instability. Root causes include GPU batch-invariance and floating-point non-associativity, not just sampling temperature.
  - Relevance: Directly relevant to the reproducibility of our LLM-in-the-loop labels: gpt-4.1-mini and FinBERT outputs are not guaranteed bit-identical on re-run, which is a reproducibility caveat for any pipeline built on hosted LLM labels. This motivates freezing/caching labels and the frozen keyword taxonomy in our design. Our study should cite this when discussing reproducibility limitations; it is a caveat we inherit, not one we solve.
- **Korinek, A. (2023). "Language Models and Cognitive Automation for Economic Research." NBER Working Paper No. 30957. Verified via NBER abstract page.**
  - Finding: Provides a taxonomy of 25 LLM use cases across six research domains (ideation, writing, background research, data analysis, coding, math), rating each from 'experimental' to 'highly useful,' and argues researchers who automate micro-tasks become substantially more productive — framing LLMs as augmenting assistants/collaborators rather than autonomous agents.
  - Relevance: Defines the 'augmented research' framing that characterizes our study: a human-designed, pre-registered pipeline where the LLM automates discrete sub-tasks (text labeling, sentiment) rather than driving the research. It situates how an 'AI boost' is credited — as a productivity tool inside a human-owned method — which matches how our LLM components are positioned (instruments, not authors). We are a concrete finance instantiation of his 'data analysis' + 'background research' categories.
- **Lu, C., Lu, C., Lange, R. T., Foerster, J., Clune, J., & Ha, D. (2024). "The AI Scientist: Towards Fully Automated Open-Ended Scientific Discovery." arXiv:2408.06292 (Sakana AI). Verified via arXiv abstract page. (A v2, arXiv:2504.08066, extends to agentic tree search and a workshop-accepted paper.)**
  - Finding: An end-to-end system in which LLMs autonomously generate ideas, write/run experiment code, analyze results, and write full papers reviewed by an automated reviewer, at <$15/paper, claiming near-conference-quality output.
  - Relevance: Represents the AUTONOMOUS pole of the autonomous-vs-augmented axis — the explicit contrast to our work. We deliberately occupy the augmented pole: humans pre-registered the hypotheses and methods BEFORE results, the LLM only supplies measurements, and the taxonomy refinement after observing 2025 episodes is honestly labelled exploratory/hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory. Citing it lets us position our anti-look-ahead, pre-registered rigor as the human-in-the-loop discipline that fully autonomous pipelines are critiqued for lacking.
- **Si, C., Yang, D., & Hashimoto, T. (2024). "Can LLMs Generate Novel Research Ideas? A Large-Scale Human Study with 100+ NLP Researchers." arXiv:2409.04109 (subsequently ICLR 2025). Verified via arXiv abstract page.**
  - Finding: In a blind study with 100+ expert NLP researchers, LLM-generated research ideas were judged significantly MORE novel (p<0.05) than expert human ideas but slightly weaker on feasibility; human re-ranking of AI ideas improved feasibility.
  - Relevance: The most-cited empirical anchor for the 'AI boost to research ideation' question and for how AI vs human contributions are framed/credited. It bounds the AI's role to ideation (and flags feasibility/self-evaluation weaknesses), reinforcing why downstream human-designed validation — as in our pre-registered, FDR-controlled finance pipeline — remains essential. Tangential to our methods but useful for the meta-science framing of where LLM augmentation helps vs where human design must dominate.

**Gap we fill:** The meta-science literature on LLM-assisted research is dominated by two framings: (a) the AUTONOMOUS-scientist vision (Sakana 'AI Scientist'), and (b) general productivity/assistant taxonomies and ideation studies (Korinek; Si-Yang-Hashimoto). Reproducibility concerns are mostly discussed in the abstract (non-determinism benchmarks like Atil et al.) or as econometric prescriptions (Ludwig-Mullainathan-Rambachan: 'use a validation sample, avoid leakage'). What is rarely DEMONSTRATED — especially in quantitative finance — is a concrete, fully AUGMENTED (human-driven, LLM-as-measurement-instrument) pipeline that operationalizes those prescriptions end to end with explicit anti-look-ahead/anti-circularity engineering: text-only labels with NO forward returns ever entering a label, a causal 7-day novelty metric, a frozen keyword taxonomy, pre-registration of hypotheses and methods BEFORE results, purged+embargoed walk-forward CV, and dual multiple-testing control (Benjamini-Hochberg FDR + Romano-Wolf).
